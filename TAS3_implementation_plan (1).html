How to reach 1,000 students — even starting from zero

1
Secure official endorsement first
Get the Uni Abuja Students Union to officially recognise and co-sign TAS 3.0. This gives you access to their mailing lists, announcement boards, and their credibility with students who don't yet know you. Visit the SUG office in week one.
2
Build a faculty ambassador network
Identify 1–2 connected, respected students in each faculty (Law, Engineering, Social Sciences, Business, Medicine, Arts, etc.). These become your ground troops. They get personalised ambassador kits, a referral code, and a WhatsApp group with you. Each ambassador targets 50–80 students in their faculty = 500–800 from ambassadors alone.
3
Host mini "Forge sessions" before the main event
Run 2–3 small community events in June/July: a panel discussion in a lecture hall, a workshop on building something in Africa, or even a movie screening + discussion. These pre-events are free, low-stakes, and build trust — people who attend these will show up in September and bring others.
4
Expand to partner universities
Reach out to student leaders at NOUN, ABUAD, BSU, NDA, Nasarawa State University, and other Abuja-area campuses. Each partner uni can contribute 50–100 attendees. Coordinate group transport (bus hire) to remove the friction of getting to the venue.
5
Run a referral-powered registration system
Every person who registers gets a unique referral link. Top 10 referrers get special recognition on the day — a badge, shoutout on stage, or a preferred seat. This turns every attendee into a recruiter. Target: each person brings 2 others on average.
6
Aggressive social media + content strategy
Post daily in August — countdowns, speaker reveals (one per week), behind-the-scenes Forge content, testimonials from TAS 1.0/2.0 attendees, grant recipient stories. Run Instagram and TikTok reels that are share-worthy — funny, bold, emotional. Consider a small paid ads budget targeting 18–30 year olds in Abuja on Instagram.
7
Physical campus presence
Put up posters and flyers in strategic spots (library, café, hostel corridors, lecture theatres). Station Forge members at peak traffic points (after morning lectures, during lunch) to talk to students directly and register them on the spot via a phone. Physical presence builds word-of-mouth in ways social media cannot.
8
Create a reason to bring a friend
Frame the summit as something you attend with your crew. "Bring a friend and both of you get priority seating." Design it so people feel social pressure — if everyone in their circle is going, FOMO does the recruitment work for you.

The empowerment grant — beyond survival

The grant is not charity — it is fuel for people already in motion. Recipients must have a proof of concept and demonstrated traction in solving an African problem.
Target: 3–5 grant recipients per edition, publicised on stage and on all media channels.
Grant value: ₦200K–₦1M per recipient depending on funding raised (total pool ₦2–5M).
Recipients also receive 6 months of mentorship, connections to sponsors, and featured storytelling on TAS platforms.

Selection criteria

📌
Proof of concept
Working prototype, MVP, or documented evidence the idea functions.
📈
Traction
Real users, community uptake, partnerships, or revenue — even small.
🌍
African problem
Clearly addresses a documented challenge faced by Africans.
📐
Scale potential
Roadmap shows how additional funding directly accelerates impact.
👤
Student-led
Led by a current or recent (≤3 years) university student or graduate.
🔁
Long-term mindset
Founders demonstrate commitment beyond personal financial survival.

Grant timeline

1
Applications open — June
Online application form (Notion or Google Form). Promote on all channels and through campus ambassadors. Accept video pitches (max 3 mins) + written summary.
2
Screening — July
Forge programs team reviews all applications. Shortlist 10–15 candidates for virtual pitches to the selection panel (include a sponsor rep and external mentor).
3
Finalists announced — August
Announce 5–8 finalists publicly. Build content around their stories — this is powerful marketing for the summit itself. Each finalist is a reason to attend.
4
Live pitch & awards — Summit day, September
Finalists pitch on the main stage (5 mins each). Audience votes + panel decision. Awards ceremony is the emotional highlight of the summit — this moment must be filmed and shared everywhere.

Key rule: Always register 20% more than your capacity target — no-shows are real. Aim for 1,200 registrations to guarantee 1,000 through the door.
On-day access control: Use a QR code ticketing system (free tools: Tix.Africa, Eventbrite Nigeria, or a custom Google Form with QR). Place 3–4 accreditation points to prevent bottlenecks.
Livestream: Stream on YouTube or Instagram Live for those who cannot attend physically — this also creates social proof and FOMO that drives next year's physical attendance.
Venue confirmation: The main hall at Uni Abuja (or the Nnamdi Azikiwe Library complex) typically holds 800–1,500. Confirm capacity and contingency overflow space with the university administration early — this is the most time-sensitive action item.
